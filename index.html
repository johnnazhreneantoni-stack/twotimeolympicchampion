Ulugbek Rashitov

Ulugbek Rashitov is a decorated Uzbekistani taekwondo practitioner who made history as the first athlete from Uzbekistan to win two Olympic gold medals in the sport. Competing in the men's 68 kg weight category, he first rose to international prominence at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics (held in 2021), where he entered as a 17th seed and secured a historic gold medal after defeating top-ranked opponents. He successfully defended his title at the Paris 2024 Olympics, defeating Zaid Kareem in the final to become a back-to-back champion. Beyond his Olympic success, Rashitov has won gold at the 2022 Asian Games and the 2019 Military World Games, and he is a student at the National University of Uzbekistan.
